12 Longest Winning Streak In NBA [2023 Update]

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, April 30, 2024

12 Longest Winning Streak in NBA History!! Winning streaks are a significant achievement in the NBA, as they consistently demonstrate a team’s ability to perform at a high level over an extended period.

One of the most impressive achievements in the league is having a long winning streak, where a team wins a consecutive number of games without losing.

Over the years, several NBA teams have put together impressive winning streaks, some of which have set records that still stand today.

Milwaukee Bucks is having a great season thus far with 16 consecutive wins highest of any team; the last win against Orlando Magic was on March 1, 2023.

In this list, we will look at the 12 longest winning streaks in NBA history, including the teams that achieved them and the seasons in which they occurred.

12. Chicago Bulls

The 1995-1996 Chicago Bulls are widely regarded as having one of the longest winning streaks in NBA history with 18 wins.

Led by the legendary Michael Jordan, the Bulls finished the regular season with a record of 72-10, which at the time was the best regular season record in the history of the NBA.

However, the team was defeated by Denver Nuggets on February 4, 1996, with a 99-105 score. 

Jordan won his eighth scoring title, averaging 30.4 points per game, and was named the league’s Most Valuable Player for the fourth time in his career.

He was joined by fellow All-Stars Scottie Pippen and Dennis Rodman, who provided excellent defense and rebounding for the team.

11. Milwaukee Bucks

During the 2019-2020 NBA season, the Milwaukee Bucks went on an impressive 18-game winning streak, one of the best in the NBA.

The streak began on November 10, 2019, and ended on December 16, 2019, when they lost to the Dallas Mavericks.

During the streak, the Bucks scored an average of 121.6 points per game, one of the highest scoring averages during a winning streak in NBA history.

It helped the Bucks establish themselves as one of the top teams in the league and ultimately led to them finishing the regular season with the best record.

10. Phoenix Suns

During the 2020-2021 NBA season, the Phoenix Suns had an impressive winning streak of 18 games, which is tied for the 10th-longest winning streak in the history of the NBA.

Their first victory was on October 30, 2021, when they defeated the Cavaliers 64-18. 

The streak helped the Suns secure a playoff spot and ultimately made them to the NBA finals for the first time since 1993.

The Suns’ success in the 2020-2021 season was due to their talented roster, including All-Stars Devin Booker and Chris Paul, as well as their strong team chemistry and coaching.

9. Los Angeles Lakers

The Los Angeles Lakers had a 19-game winning streak during the 1999-2000 NBA season.

This streak began on February 4, 2000, with a victory over the Utah Jazz with 113-67 points and ended on March 13, 2000, with a loss to the Washington Wizards.

During this winning streak, the Lakers defeated several quality opponents, including the Utah Jazz, Portland Trail Blazers, San Antonio Spurs, and Phoenix Suns.

The Lakers won the NBA championship that season, with Shaquille O’Neal winning the NBA Finals MVP award.

At the same time, 21 years old Kobe Bryant was named All-NBA Defensive First Team while emerging as one of the NBA’s young superstars.

8. Boston Celtics

The Boston Celtics’ 19-game winning streak during the 2008-2009 NBA season is tied for the eighth-longest in NBA history.

Led by superstars Kevin Garnett, Paul Pierce, and Ray Allen, the Celtics won 19 consecutive games between November 15, 2008, and December 23, 2008.

The streak began with a 102-97 win over the Milwaukee Bucks and ended with an 83-92 loss to the Los Angeles Lakers.

The Celtics completed the regular season with a record of 62-20, the best in the Eastern Conference, but lost to the Orlando Magic in the Eastern Conference semifinals.

7. San Antonio Spurs

The 2013-2014 Spurs team is considered one of the greatest in NBA history, and their 19-game winning streak is a testament to their incredible talent, teamwork, and coaching.

Future Hall of Famers Tim Duncan, Tony Parker, and Manu Ginobili led the Spurs to win 19 consecutive games.

The streak began with a 120-110 win over the Pistons and ended with a 106-94 loss to the Thunder.

The Spurs finished the regular season with a 62-20, the best in the league, and reached the NBA finals, where they ended up being the season’s runner-up.

6. Atlanta Hawks

The Atlanta Hawks had a successful 2014-2015 season, finishing with a record of 60-22 against the Bucks.

They won the Southeast Division and made it to the Eastern Conference Finals, where the Cavaliers defeated them.

The team was led by their All-Star duo Jeff Teague and power forward Paul Millsap.

The 2014-2015 season was memorable for the Atlanta Hawks as it is the sixth-longest winning streak with 19 in the NBA. 

5. Milwaukee Bucks

During the 1970-1971 NBA season, the Milwaukee Bucks put together a 20-game winning streak, the fifth-longest in NBA history.

Led by star center Kareem Abdul-Jabbar (then known as Lew Alcindor) and point guard Oscar Robertson, the Bucks won 20 consecutive games between February 6, 1971, and March 8, 1971.

The streak began with a 111-85 victory over the San Francisco Warriors and ended with a 103-110 defeat to the Bulls.

The Bucks finished the regular season with a record of 66-16, the best in the league, and won the season finals against New York Knicks.

4. Houston Rockets

The Houston Rockets’ 22-game winning streak during the 2007-2008 NBA season is the fourth-longest in NBA history.

Tracy McGrady and Hall Of Famer Yao Ming were great players for the Houston Rockets, who contributed 12 wins to their team before Ming broke his foot and was sidelined.

Throughout the middle of the 2000s, the Houston Rockets were among the league’s most entertaining teams to watch.

The streak began with a 111-107 win over the Warriors and ended with a 74-94 loss to the Celtics. 

However, their 22-game winning streak remains a testament to their dominance during that memorable season.

3. Miami Heat

During the 2012-2013 NBA season, the Miami Heat put together an incredible 27-game winning streak, the third-longest in NBA history.

Led by superstars LeBron James, Dwyane Wade, and Chris Bosh, the Heat won 27 straight games between February 3, 2013, and March 25, 2013.

The streak began with a 100-85 win over the Raptors and ended with a 101-97 loss to the Bulls.

The Heat finished the regular season with a record of 66-16, the best in the league.

They went on to win their second consecutive NBA championship, defeating the San Antonio Spurs in a thrilling seven-game series.

2. Golden State Warriors

The Golden State Warriors had a 28-game winning streak during the 2015-2016 NBA season. This streak began on November 28, 2015, with a victory over the Kings and ended on January 27, 2016, with a loss to the Bucks.

The 2016 NBA All-Star Game featured Stephen Curry, Draymond Green, and Klay Thompson. Green surpassed the nine triple-double season record held by Golden State as a team.

Curry shattered several three-point records throughout the season, including his personal NBA record of 286 made three-pointers in a season. He concluded the campaign with 402 three-pointers.

During this winning streak, the Warriors set several records, including the best start to a season in NBA history with 24 consecutive wins.

The Warriors set a new regular-season record of 73 wins and nine losses but ultimately lost to the Cavaliers in the finals.

1. Los Angeles Lakers

The Los Angeles Lakers of the 1971-1972 season hold the record for the longest winning streak in NBA history, with an impressive 33 consecutive victories.

On November 5, 1971, they beat the Baltimore Bullets by 4 points (110–106), and that was how they got their start.

It persisted well through the holidays and the beginning of the New Year before being finally broken on January 9, 1972, by Kareem and Oscar’s Milwaukee Bucks.

The game was led by Hall of Famers Wilt Chamberlain, Jerry West, and Gail Goodrich. The Lakers finished the regular season with a 69-13 record, the best in the league that year.

Later they won the NBA championship, defeating the New York Knicks in the finals.

Conclusion

We have reached the end of our article on the 12 longest-winning streaks in NBA history.

These winning streaks are a testament to the skill and determination of the teams that achieved them. They also serve as a reminder of the incredible talent and competition in the NBA.
