Sophie Ellis-Bextor finally ends 20-year feud with Robbie Williams after he said she had face 'like

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, January 31, 2024

SOPHIE Ellis-Bextor has buried the hatchet with Robbie Williams 20-years after he said her face "looked like a satellite dish".

The singer 39-year-old and The X Factor judge, 44, fell out after Sophie declined a support slot on Robbie's first solo tour.

Rubbing further salt into Robbie's wounds, Sophie labelled him a "tart" and a "prat" leading Robbie to hit back with an insult of his own.

Getting personal he said Sophie had "a face like a satellite dish and my nan’s ankles," before going on to apologise.

Speaking to the Daily Mail, Sophie said: "I think I need to apologise to him.

"We had this slightly weird altercation when I hadn’t really worked out that there are things you say that sound quite funny to your mates but in black and white it just doesn’t look good.

"He was right to be cross. I have apologised to him once before, and he was really sweet about it, but I’ll do it again one day. I’m not very proud of it."

In mature, self-deprecating fashion, she added: "He was sort of right about them – they are granny’s ankles… but it’s my granny."

While the bad blood may be no more, Sophie isn't watching Robbie in his new role on the ITV talent show, instead she prefers Strictly.

Robbie is so committed to his X Factor gig that he got a tribute tattooed on his skin earlier this week.

Louis Tomlinson joined Robbie in the studio to get matching 'X' symbols, with mixed results.

While Louis, 26, expertly inked Robbie, Rob royally messed up the ex-One Direction star's tat - leaving him with something that resembled a religious cross rather than the X Factor 'X'.

X Factor's Robbie Williams and Louis Tomlinson tattoo each other with matching symbols ahead of live shows

Sharing footage of the shenanigans to his latest YouTube vlog, Robbie could be seen apologising profusely to his co-star.

With his wife Ayda Field looking on, Robbie was the first to go under the needle, and Louis warned the singer: "I ain't got a steady hand by the way.

"I'm sorry in advance."

However, Rob brushed off Louis' concerns, telling him: "It's all good - whatever it turns out like, I'm not bothered."

He was left relatively pleased with the final result, telling worried Louis that "it's got personality".

Unfortunately for Lou, Robbie wasn't quite as skilled with the needle.

As he grimaced in pain, Louis yelped: "Dig deep why don't you, f***ing hell, Rob."

Once he'd seen Robbie's first attempt, the nervous 1D star commented: "I think we are going to have to make it a bit thicker... I look religious all of a sudden."

It was apparent that an apologetic Robbie was getting more and more flustered, but when he offered for the professional tattoo artist to finish the job for him, Louis refused, saying: "You fix it, lad.

"I believe in you."

Eventually Robbie gave up, with Louis attempting to alleviate his guilt, insisting: "F*** it, none of my tattoos are nice.

"I've got a lot of bad tattoos... But that now is by far the craziest."
