Shameless star Jody Latham's business he boasted was worth 18m now has just 1 in the bank

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, October 2, 2024

SHAMELESS actor Jody Latham has seen his self-proclaimed "£18m cosmetics empire" and "multi-millionaire" status falter according to financial records.

The TV star, 39, who played Philip ‘Lip’ Gallagher in the Channel 4 series, previously told how he got into the beauty business when he met an extra who was doing Botox injections.

Jody then proudly claimed his firm Epitique had signed a money-spinning deal to supply dermal fillers for cosmetic treatments to a firm in South Korea.

Yet The Sun Online can now exclusively reveal how things appear to have gone south for the Shameless favourite just last month.

We can report, now that his accounts have been filed, the scenario is much less flash.

In fact documents reveal Epitique UK Ltd, the business attached to it, is now dormant.

The only set of accounts from February 2022 state the firm has just £1 in its accounts.

This may explain why, despite his success, Jody previously claimed that he isn't splashing out too much on a lavish lifestyle.

He said: “I live in a two-bed flat and drive a modest car. I’m pumping everything back into the company.

“It’s valued at £18million. We’ll see where we are in a couple of years. I’ve had money before but not in my hands long enough.”

BUSINESS ROLLERCOASTER

Unfortunately, previous companies set up by Jody have also struggled.

He set up Intraline UK Ltd – described as being involved in physical well-being activities – in 2018 but it was dissolved without ever filing any accounts to Companies House the following year.

UK Aesthetics (Wholesale) Ltd was formed in May 2018 and dissolved in October 2019 via a compulsory strike off for not filing official documents like accounts.

And UK Aesthetics (Academy) Ltd was incorporated in 2018 - accounts filed for this firm in July show zero profit and a debt of £399.

Deansgate Aesthetics Ltd was set up in 2016 and dissolved two years later via a compulsory strike off without filing books.

UK Fillers Ltd – created in 2015 and dissolved in 2019 – filed its last accounts in 2018 where it was shown the firm owed £3,615.

And a firm called Lady Bomb Ltd – said to be linked to artistic creation – ceased trading in 2017.

REHAB STINT

Jody's business brain was somewhat distracted in 2020 as the star prioritised his mental health during the coronavirus pandemic.

Jody told James English’s Anything Goes podcast: “I did a stint in rehab - I’ve never spoken about this publicly. It wasn’t for me - but I learned a lot from it.

"My substance abuse is minimal, I do drink sometimes, not every day and not a lot, but because I was going through anxiety and business worries, there was an opportunity to go and refresh.

"It was exhaustion as well. I took a week out, it was going to be a month, but I took a week.”

He continued: “I looked at doing AA but it’s all on video, Zoom and all that, so it wasn’t really for me.
