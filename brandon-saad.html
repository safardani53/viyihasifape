Brandon Saad | Amateur Years

The professional NHL player Brandon Saad has been playing as an ice hockey player since his childhood.

Though not much is given regarding his early career, Brandon played in the 2005 Quebec International Fee-Wee Hockey Tournament. 

Likewise, he played the tournament with the Pittsburgh Hornets minor ice hockey team. Then in the 2008-09 season, Saad played for the Mahoning Valley Phantoms that kick-started his amateur ice hockey career.

In his next season, Brandon played within the USA Hockey National Team Development Program in the United States Hockey League(USHL).

Not to mention, Saad finished his season with the most goals, i.e., 12 and 26 points on the team.

Similarly, Brandon transferred to the Canadian Hockey League(CHL) for the following season and played at the major junior level with the Saginaw Spirit of the Ontario Hockey League(OHL). 

By the end of the season, Bradon made it to the Chicago Blackhawks, who picked him in the second round, 43rd overall, at the 2011 NHL Entry Draft.

Right after his selection, the Chicago Blackhawks signed a three-year entry-level contract on October 4, 2011.

He also made history by being the first of the lowest-selected Chicago draft pick to make his NHL debut.

For this debut game, Brandon starred against the Dallas Stars. He was quickly reassigned to Saginaw five days later.

From October 17 to October 23, Saad was named CHL Player of the Week, then called captain for the remainder of the 2011-12 season. 

After the end of the OHL season, Saad then got back with the Chicago Blackhawks team and made his Stanley Cup playoff debut on April 19, 2012.
