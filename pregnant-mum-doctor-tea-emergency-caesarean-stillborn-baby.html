Doctor told mum to wait until they had TEA before performing emergency Caesarean on baby who died mo

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, February 16, 2024

A DOCTOR told a mum-to-be to wait until she had tea before performing an emergency Caesarean.

The baby died moments after being born, after the expectant mum was also told to have a shower before the surgery.

The doctor was chastised by coroner John Pollard during the inquest into baby Rueben Winrow Monks' death.

He told consultant obstetrician and gynaecologist Mrinal Shah: "You were not up to the job, were you?"

Mrs Shah said she had been concerned about her blood sugar level and needed to have tea, when emotional Laura Monks required the surgery.

Bolton Coroners Court heard Rueben was delivered at Royal Albert Edward Infirmary in Wigan, in November 2011.

The hospital trust has already admitted responsibility for failings and his parents, Laura and Peter Winrow had earlier received a £40 000 pay out after inadequacies at the Wrightington, Wigan and Leigh NHS Foundation Trust.

The coroner asked: “Why on earth would you send a patient in this state to have a shower and why on earth would you need your tea before performing an emergency Caesarean section?

"I find it phenomenally unbelievable that you would pop off for a spot of tea when you should have been whisking her straight off for surgery.”

Mrs Shah apologised to the inquest and agreed she was not assertive enough.

Jonathan Robinson, a consultant paediatrician, told the inquest: "When Reuben was 44 mins old, I detected a brief heart rate of 30-40 bpm.

“This was after the injection of a lot of adrenaline and there was cardiac activity. There’s was no pulse, and very little sign of life."

Ms Monks, 33, from Wigan, revealed after the hearing she had blamed herself after the death of her son, and had for years believed that her own body had let him down.

She says: “I began to believe I wasn’t a fit mother and even questioned whether I was able to look after my older son, Darcy, who is now 10.”

She and her partner, Peter, 37, also have a daughter, Niamh, now five.

Laura says: “It was a wonderful moment to bring Niamh home and to know she was OK. But that doesn’t change what happened to Rueben.

“For years, we were given no reason for Rueben’s death, no explanation. They said it was ‘just one of those things.’

The inquest, listed for three days, is expected to conclude tomorrow.

After admitted their failings, a spokesman for the Wrightington, Wigan and Leigh NHS Foundation Trust apologised to the family.

Adding: “Following Rueben’s tragic death an investigation was commenced by the Trust to ensure lessons were learnt to improve the care that we provide. The Trust is committed to providing an excellent service and we recognise the importance of learning from mistakes of this nature.

Rueben’s death is now subject to an HM Coroner’s investigation. The Trust is unable to comment further whilst coronial proceedings are ongoing.”
