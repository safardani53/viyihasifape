Illegal cigarettes that could contain dead flies and even human EXCREMENT sold over the counter in L

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, March 31, 2024

THIS is the moment illegal cigarettes are sold over the counter in London – with warnings the black market trade that sells fags containing dead flies and human excrement to unsuspecting buyers could worsen with the introduction of plain packaging.

More than a third of London's smokers have purchased illegal tobacco, some of which include the stomach-churning ingredients of asbestos, mould, dust and rat droppings, from retailers around the city, according to new research.

In never-before-seen footage, undercover shoppers have now captured the ease at which they can buy the cigarettes from over the counter, with the cartons costing as little as £3.

One shopper can be asking for the “cheap cigarettes”, charged £5.50 for a packet.

In the covert filming, the man then hands over the cash, while in another case, the woman buys a magazine and some illegal cigarettes with no problems.

One in eight of more than 400 London retailers who were mystery shopped were found to be selling illegal cigarettes and tobacco.

An independent party conducted the tests, finding specific hot spots across London included Kilburn, Maida Vale, Turnpike Lane and Brick Lane, with the shops mostly convenience stores.

Illegal tobacco has been linked to organised crime, as well as terrorist financing.

Japan Tobacco International has now flagged concerns that the branding ban on cigarettes could see a rise in illegal tobacco, with it easier for criminals to produce counterfeit cigarette packages and mislead customers.

The cheap price also makes it attractive to young and vulnerable people.

With the decreasing size of cigarette packages, there are further fears that smokers will be driven to the black market for cheaper smokes.

Steve Wilkins, JTI’s Anti-Illegal Trade Operations Director and former Detective Chief Superintendent at Dyfed Powys Police said: "Smokers buying cheap fake cigarettes and tobacco on the streets of the UK may be getting more than they bargain for as these fake imitations has been found to contain asbestos, mould, dust, dead flies, rat droppings and even human excrement.

"The availability of cheap illegal tobacco across towns and cities in the UK is damaging local communities and along with retailers, suppliers and the Government we all have a role to play to combat the issue."

How to spot illegal tobacco

  • Unrecognisable names, e.g.: Jin Ling, Raquel, Gold Classic, Richman, Capital, Camelford
  • Foreign language health warning
  • No picture in the health warning
  • Unusual taste or smell
  • Prices usually less than £3.50 for a pack of 20

*According to Crimestoppers UK

Roger Critchell, Crimestoppers Director of Operations, added: "As a crime-fighting Charity, Crimestoppers has a responsibility to tackle all types of crime, and working alongside JTI UK, we hope to be able to clean the streets of illicit tobacco.

"The link this trade has to serious and organised crime is very real, and with the help of information from the public on those supplying and selling these fake products, our aim is to ensure your community is a safe place to live."

An HMRC spokesperson said: "The UK faces a continued threat from cheap illicit tobacco products. The majority of retailers are law abiding traders.

"However, there are those who believe they can make easy money by selling illicit tobacco products under the counter. HMRC continues to develop and refine the methods used to combat the ever-changing nature of illicit trade."
