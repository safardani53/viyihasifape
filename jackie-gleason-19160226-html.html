John Herbert Gleason is an American actor, comedian, writer, composer and conductor affectionately known as "The Great". Developing his style and character after growing up in Brooklyn, New York, he is known for his sharp visual and verbal comedy, portrayed as city bus driver Ralph Kramden in the series TV series The Honeymooners. He also developed The Jackie Gleason Show, which maintained high ratings from the mid-1950s to 1970s. After coming from New York City, the studio moved to Miami Beach, Florida in 1964 after Gleason planned. permanently reside there. Among his notable film roles were Minnesota Fats in The Hustler and Buford T. Justice in the Smokey and the Bandit series from 1977 to the early 1980s. During the 1950s and 1960s, Gleason had a prominent supporting music career, producing a series of successful "soundtrack" albums.
