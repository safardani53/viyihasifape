Is John Cusack from Chicago and is he a White Sox or Cubs fan?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, March 18, 2024

CHICAGO sports fans are known for their die-hard allegiance to their team of choice.

Actor John Cusack got into it with Barstool Sports employee Dave Williams following a Chicago White Sox game on Sunday, October 10.

Who is John Cusack?

Born June 28, 1966, John Cusack is a well-known American actor.

His debut into acting was in 1983, with the comedy Class. He's known for his role in movies such as Sixteen Candles and The Sure Thing, which was his first leading role.

Cusack has starred in over 85 films, and has never been married.

His family includes father Dick Cusack, an American filmmaker, and sisters Joan and Ann Cusack, both actresses.

Cusack also trained under former world kickboxing champion Benny Urquidez for over 20 years, starting when he got his role in Say Anything in 1989. He is a level six black belt.

Is John Cusack from Chicago?

John Cusack was born in Evanston, Illinois.

Evanston is a suburb northwest of Chicago, and is home to Northwestern University.

Attending Nicolas Junior High School, Cusack starred in a multitude of local stage productions. At age 9, he attended Piven Theatre Workshop in Evanston, and went on to become a well-known voiceover actor.

Cusack graduated from Evanston Township High School in 1984, before spending one year at New York University.

Is John Cusack a White Sox or Cubs fan?

On Sunday, October 10, 2021, John Cusack attended a White Sox game in Chicago, Illinois.

Barstool Sports employee, Dave Williams, who goes by White Sox Dave, confronted Cusack outside of the Guaranteed Rate Field.

He accused Cusack of pledging allegiance to whichever Chicago team was doing better at the time, switching between the Cubs and the White Sox.

Cusack responded by saying he's a fan of both teams, and grew up going to both ballparks.

Williams didn't seem to enjoy this answer, calling Cusack a bandwagon fan at one point.

The two "agree to disagree."

Cusack has reportedly been seen wearing both Cubs and White Sox gear, and has even tweeted about the ordeal in the past.
