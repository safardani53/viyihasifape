It's iPhone 7 launch day tomorrow - here's where to buy one, how much it costs and how to get the be

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, April 18, 2024

THE new iPhone will be released tomorrow after a highly anticipated launch last week.

And Apple fans are already queuing up to get their hands on the new device - with reports that someone started queuing outside New York's Fifth Avenue store since August 25.

The latest handset features a dual-lens camera and is waterproof - and comes installed with iOS10, Apple's newest software update, as standard.

Controversially, Apple have also done away with the usual headphone jack - and headphones will now be connected using the lighting connection.

But how much does it cost? Well, to buy an iPhone 7 outright from Apple, it'll set you back at least £599 - or £719 if you opt for the iPhone 7 Plus.

How to trade in your old handset for an iPhone 7 and get up to £320 in cash

The most expensive iPhone 7 model, with 128GB of storage space, costs a whopping £799 - and the 256GB iPhone 7 Plus is an unbelievable £919.

However, mobile networks are offering a number of contract deals - some with no upfront costs.

For example, Tesco Mobile is offering the 32GB iPhone 7 for £44.75 a month with no upfront costs - and 5000 minutes, 5000 texts and 3GB of data. The same deal for the iPhone 7 Plus costs £50 a month.

Meanwhile, the 32GB iPhone 7 with 5000 minutes, unlimited texts and 5GB of data on Virgin Media will set you back £47 a month.

To get the best deals, it's worth using a comparison website which breaks down all of the different options by the factors which matter most to you - such as having unlimited data, or deals which include a free phone.

Some sites, such as uSwitch, also have exclusive offers only available through their site - such as £47 a month for the 32GB iPhone 7 with unlimited minutes, unlimited texts and 12GB of data on Vodafone.
