Driver's fury after parking fine slapped on car where permit was hidden by SNOW

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, May 18, 2024

A DRIVER has told of his fury after he was slapped with a parking fine – because his permit was hidden by snow.

Oliver Claxton from Derby claims the council then uploaded pics of the snow-covered motor as proof that he didn’t have a valid permit on display.

Oliver said he returned from a work trip away in London to discover he had been fined.

He said he was shocked as he had never moved the permit from his car, so he decided to challenge Derby City Council, BBC Radio Derby reported.

Oliver, who works at the BBC himself, said he thought it was “odd” that he had only been fined for one day if he hadn’t properly displayed his permit – as he had left his vehicle there the whole time he was out of town.

So he said went on the council website to look for clues as to why the local authority had seen fit to punish him.

He said: "I was thinking, ‘what could it possibly be that meant they couldn't see the permit on this particular day?’

"When you go on the website they have the photographs they took and the reason the permit wasn't visible was because it had snowed overnight and the windscreen was covered in snow.

"I just laughed. It was ridiculous."

Parking regulations state that in areas where permits are required to park, they must be valid and clearly displayed.

But Oliver claimed that if the parking warden had just swept the snow away, they would easily have been able to see the permit.

And the photos appear to prove that no such effort was made.

Derby City Council said: "We're not able to comment on individual cases but we are looking into the challenge and will be responding to the individual directly soon."
